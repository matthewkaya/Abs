    Data Processing Agreement (DPA)¶

    Between

    Automatia ABS, S.L. (hereinafter "Processor"), a company incorporated under the laws of Spain, with registered office at C/ Gran Via, 123, 08008 Barcelona, Spain, and

    [Customer Name] (hereinafter "Controller"), a legal entity organized under the laws of [Country], with registered office at [Address].

    Effective Date: [Date]**

    1. Preamble – Parties and Background¶

    1.1 The Processor provides the Automatia Billing Suite (ABS) – a cloud‑based software platform that enables the Controller to manage licensing, invoicing, subscription billing, and technical support for its own customers.

    1.2 In the course of providing the ABS, the Processor will process personal data on behalf of the Controller in accordance with the Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council (General Data Protection Regulation – "GDPR") and any applicable national legislation of Spain.

    1.3 The parties wish to set out, in a legally binding manner, the terms governing such processing, the respective responsibilities, and the safeguards to be applied, in order to comply with Article 28 of the GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 (UK) where relevant.

    Accordingly, the parties agree as follows.

    2. Definitions¶

    For the purpose of this DPA, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them in Article 4 of the GDPR unless otherwise defined herein:

    Term Definition
    Personal data Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
    Processing Any operation or set of operations performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means (e.g., collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, use, disclosure, erasure, etc.).
    Controller The natural or legal person, public authority, agency or any other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data.
    Processor A natural or legal person, public authority, agency or any other body which processes personal data on behalf of the controller.
    Sub‑processor Any third‑party engaged by the Processor to carry out specific processing activities on behalf of the Controller.
    Data subject An identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data are processed.
    Data protection law The GDPR, any national implementing legislation, and any other applicable data‑protection regulations.
    Breach A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data.
    Technical and organisational measures (TOMs) Measures designed to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure or access.
    Supervisory Authority The independent public authority established pursuant to Article 51 of the GDPR.

    3. Subject Matter, Duration and Scope of Processing¶

    Item Description
    3.1 Subject Matter The Processor shall provide the Controller with access to the ABS platform, including licensing, billing, invoicing, subscription management, usage‑based analytics, and technical‑support services. The Processor will process the Controller's personal data solely for the purpose of delivering those services.
    3.2 Duration This DPA shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in force for the entire term of the underlying service agreement (the "Service Agreement") between the parties, unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 9.
    3.3 Licensing The Processor grants the Controller a non‑exclusive, worldwide, non‑transferable right to use the ABS software, subject to the terms of the Service Agreement and this DPA.
    3.4 Billing & Payment The Processor shall generate invoices, process payments, and retain billing‑related personal data (see Section 5).
    3.5 Audit & Monitoring The Controller may, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours, conduct an audit of the Processor's compliance with this DPA and applicable data‑protection law. The Processor shall cooperate fully and provide all relevant documentation.
    3.6 LLM Routing Where the Controller opts to use the optional Large‑Language‑Model (LLM) routing feature, the Processor may forward anonymised usage logs to a third‑party LLM provider for the purpose of generating automated responses. Such forwarding shall be governed by the same safeguards set out herein.

    4. Nature and Purpose of the Processing¶

    1. Software Licensing & Access – Provision of a SaaS environment that enables the Controller to create, modify, and delete user accounts, assign licences, and manage entitlements.

    2. Billing & Invoicing – Generation of invoices, processing of payments, and reconciliation of financial data, including the storage of payment‑method details where required.

    3. Technical Support – Provision of help‑desk services, remote troubleshooting, and incident management, which may involve the exchange of personal data to resolve technical issues.

    4. Analytics & Reporting – Production of usage statistics, performance dashboards, and compliance reports for the Controller's internal decision‑making.

    5. LLM Routing (Optional) – Transmission of anonymised usage logs to a third‑party LLM service for the purpose of delivering AI‑driven assistance within the ABS platform.

    All processing shall be documented in the Processor's internal processing register and shall be limited to the purposes set out above.

    5. Categories of Processed Data¶

    Category Example Data Source
    5.1 Contact Information Name, email address, telephone number, postal address of the Controller's employees, customers, and partners. Directly supplied by the Controller or its end‑users.
    5.2 Usage Logs & Technical Data IP address, device identifiers, timestamps, API request/response payloads, error logs, feature‑usage metrics. Automatically generated by the ABS platform.
    5.3 Intellectual Property (IP) Data License keys, software activation codes, product serial numbers, proprietary code snippets uploaded for support. Supplied by the Controller or its customers.
    5.4 Billing & Financial Data Invoice numbers, payment amounts, credit‑card tokenised data, bank account references, tax identification numbers. Provided by the Controller for invoicing and payment processing.
    5.5 Support Interaction Data Ticket numbers, chat transcripts, screen‑share recordings, diagnostic logs. Generated during technical‑support interactions.
    5.6 LLM‑Related Data (Optional) Anonymised usage logs (e.g., request type, language, response latency). No direct identifiers. Exported automatically when the LLM routing feature is enabled.

    The Processor shall not process any special categories of personal data (as defined in Article 9 GDPR) unless expressly authorised in writing by the Controller.

    6. Processor Obligations¶

    6.1 Processing Only on Documented Instructions¶

    The Processor shall process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller, including any instructions concerning transfers of personal data to a third country or an international organisation, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law. In the latter case, the Processor shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before complying, unless that law prohibits such notification.

    6.2 Confidentiality¶

    All persons authorised by the Processor to process personal data shall be bound by a duty of confidentiality, whether contractual or statutory, and shall not disclose personal data to any third party without the Controller's prior written consent.

    6.3 Security of Processing (Art. 32 GDPR)¶

    The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures (TOMs) to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including, at a minimum:

    Measure Description
    Encryption at Rest & in Transit All personal data shall be encrypted using AES‑256 (at rest) and TLS 1.3 (in transit).
    Access Controls Role‑based access control (RBAC) with least‑privilege principle; multi‑factor authentication for privileged accounts.
    Logging & Monitoring Centralised security‑event logging, real‑time intrusion detection, and automated alerting.
    Annual Penetration Testing Independent third‑party penetration tests shall be performed at least once per calendar year, with results made available to the Controller upon request.
    Vulnerability Management Timely patching of operating systems, middleware, and application components (critical patches within 48 hours).
    Data Minimisation & Retention Retention periods shall be limited to those necessary for the purposes set out in Section 4, and data shall be securely deleted thereafter.
    Backup & Disaster Recovery Encrypted backups stored in geographically separate data centres, with a recovery time objective (RTO) of 4 hours and a recovery point objective (RPO) of 1 hour.

    The Processor shall review and, where necessary, update these measures at least annually, taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, the nature, scope, context and purposes of processing, and the risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects.

    6.4 Sub‑processors (Art. 28.2 & 28.4)¶

    1. The Processor may engage sub‑processors to carry out specific processing activities on its behalf. A current list of approved sub‑processors is maintained in the file subprocessors.md and shall be made available to the Controller upon request.

    2. The Processor shall:

    3. Notify the Controller at least 30 calendar days before engaging a new sub‑processor or making a material change to an existing sub‑processor's services.

    4. Provide the Controller with the sub‑processor's name, location, and a summary of the processing activities to be performed.

    5. Allow the Controller the right to object to any proposed sub‑processor within 10 calendar days of notification. If the Controller objects, the parties shall discuss an alternative solution in good faith.

    6. The Processor shall enter into a written contract with each sub‑processor that imposes obligations no less protective than those set out in this DPA. The Processor shall remain fully liable for any breach of those obligations by its sub‑processors.

    6.5 Data Subject Rights (DSARs)¶

    1. Assistance – The Processor shall assist the Controller, at the Controller's cost, in responding to data‑subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) received from data subjects.

    2. Response Time – The Processor shall provide the Controller with the necessary information within 10 calendar days of receiving a request from the Controller.

    3. Technical Endpoint – The Processor shall maintain a RESTful endpoint /v1/me/data-export that, upon authenticated request from the Controller, returns a machine‑readable (JSON) export of all personal data the Processor holds concerning the identified data subject, together with metadata on the processing purpose, retention period, and any disclosures to third parties.

    4. Deletion – Where a data‑subject requests erasure, the Processor shall delete the relevant data without undue delay, unless retention is required for compliance with a legal obligation.

    6.6 Personal Data Breach Notification¶

    1. Notification Deadline – The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours after becoming aware of a personal data breach.

    2. Content of Notification – The notification shall contain:

    3. A description of the nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected;

    4. The likely consequences of the breach;
    5. Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects;

    6. Contact details of the Processor's data‑protection officer (DPO) or other relevant contact point.

    7. Co‑operation – The Processor shall cooperate fully with the Controller in any investigation, remediation, and communication with supervisory authorities and affected data subjects.

    6.7 Audit Rights¶

    1. The Controller (or an auditor it appoints) shall have the right to conduct an audit of the Processor's compliance with this DPA and applicable data‑protection law once per calendar year or more frequently if a material breach is suspected.

    2. Audits shall be performed during normal business hours, with reasonable prior notice (minimum 10 calendar days). The Processor shall provide:

    3. Access to all relevant premises, systems, and documentation;

    4. Copies of policies, procedures, risk assessments, and security‑testing reports;

    5. Evidence of sub‑processor contracts and certifications.

    6. The Processor shall remediate any identified non‑compliance within 30 calendar days of receiving the audit report, unless a longer period is mutually agreed.

    6.8 Deletion / Return of Personal Data (Art. 28.3.g)¶

    1. Upon termination of the Service Agreement (for any reason) and within 30 calendar days thereafter, the Processor shall:

    2. Delete all personal data processed on behalf of the Controller, unless retention is required by Union or Member State law; or

    3. Return the personal data to the Controller in a structured, commonly used, machine‑readable format (e.g., JSON or CSV).

    4. The Processor shall provide the Controller with a certificate of deletion (or a confirmation of return) signed by an authorised officer of the Processor, confirming that all copies, backups, and logs containing personal data have been securely destroyed or returned.

    7. Sub‑processor List (Reference)¶

    A current list of sub‑processors is maintained in the file subprocessors.md and includes, but is not limited to:

    Sub‑processor Country Processing Activity
    CloudHost S.A. Spain Hosting of ABS platform (IaaS)
    LogAnalytics Ltd. Ireland Aggregation and analysis of usage logs
    PaymentGateway EU Netherlands Tokenised payment‑method storage and transaction processing
    AI‑Assist GmbH Germany LLM routing (optional feature)
    BackupSecure Inc. United Kingdom Encrypted backup and disaster‑recovery services

    The Processor shall keep this list up‑to‑date and shall notify the Controller of any additions, deletions or material changes in accordance with Section 6.4.

    8. Liability & Indemnification¶

    1. Processor Liability – The Processor shall be liable for any direct damages suffered by the Controller that are caused by the Processor's breach of its obligations under this DPA, subject to a maximum aggregate liability of €5,000,000 (five million euros) per calendar year.

    2. Controller Liability – The Controller shall be liable for any damages arising from its own breach of the DPA, including failure to provide accurate instructions or to obtain necessary consents from data subjects.

    3. Indemnity – Each party shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless the other party, its affiliates, officers, directors, employees and agents from any third‑party claim, suit, action, or proceeding arising out of:

    4. (a) the indemnifying party's breach of the GDPR or this DPA;

    5. (b) any failure by the indemnifying party to comply with its statutory obligations under applicable data‑protection law.

    6. Exclusions – Neither party shall be liable for indirect, consequential, special, punitive or exemplary damages, nor for loss of profit, goodwill, or data, except to the extent such loss results directly from a breach of confidentiality or security obligations.

    7. Force Majeure – Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, provided that the affected party promptly notifies the other party and takes reasonable steps to mitigate the impact.

    9. Term & Termination¶

    Clause Description
    9.1 Term This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Service Agreement, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this clause.
    9.2 Termination for Cause Either party may terminate this DPA with 30 calendar days' written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within that period.
    9.3 Effect of Termination Upon termination, the Processor shall, in accordance with Section 6.8, delete or return all personal data, certify such deletion/return, and cease all processing activities.
    9.4 Survival The obligations set out in Sections 6 (Processor obligations), 8 (Liability & Indemnification), and any confidentiality provisions shall survive termination for as long as the Processor retains personal data or as required by applicable law.
    9.5 Data Retention Post‑Termination If the Processor is required by law to retain certain data (e.g., for tax or accounting purposes), it shall retain only the minimum necessary and shall continue to apply the security measures set out in Section 6.3.

    10. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution¶

    1. Governing Law – This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Spain, without regard to its conflict‑of‑law principles.

    2. Jurisdiction – The courts of Barcelona, Spain shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or relating to this DPA, unless the parties agree otherwise in writing.

    3. Alternative Dispute Resolution – Prior to initiating court proceedings, the parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute through good‑faith negotiations. If negotiations fail, the parties may agree to submit the dispute to mediation administered by an accredited European mediation centre.
